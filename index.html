The Band

U2 are an Irish rock band from Dublin, Ireland. The group consists of Bono (lead vocals and rhythm guitar), the Edge (lead guitar, keyboards, and backing vocals), Adam Clayton (bass guitar), and Larry Mullen Jr. (drums and percussion). Initially rooted in post-punk, U2's musical style has evolved throughout their career, yet has maintained an anthemic quality built on Bono's expressive vocals and the Edge's effects-based guitar textures.

It all began in 1976 when Larry Mullen pinned a 'musicians wanted' ad to the notice board at Dublin's Temple Mount School: Drummer seeks musicians to form band. 'So on Saturday 25th September 1976', recalls Larry, 'This odd group of people convened in my kitchen in Artane. And that's where it started.'

Adam Clayton had discovered rock'n'roll as a thirteen year old, buying his first acoustic guitar and then talking his parents into buying him a bass guitar. 'It just sounded good to me. Deep and fat and satisfying.'

From the beginning, U2 were marked out by their passion. "A band before we could play" was how Bono put it in early interviews. Edge remembers reading UK music papers NME and Sounds every week and then hearing about this 'wild kid called Paul Hewson.'

Tours

’As a live band,’ says Edge, ’we are always looking for a magic that occurs when we play with our audience present. That's why people come back to our shows again and again.’

From a 1977 talent contest in a school cafe, when they could barely play their instruments, to the most spectacular shows in the world's biggest venues four decades later, U2 are most themselves when they are playing live.

Scroll down to see the main tours, starting from the latest.

The Joshua Tree Tour 2019

5 Countries / 15 Shows

The tour visited Oceania and Asia in November and December, marking the band's first ever concerts in South Korea, Singapore, the Philippines and India. In December 2019 the band played Mumbai in India, the final show of The Joshua Tree Tour 2019. And the final show of four years of touring between 2015 and 2020.

Experience + Innocence Tour 2018

11 Countries / 60 Shows

The Experience + Innocence Tour was staged in support of the band's 2017 album, Songs of Experience. The tour, comprising two legs and 60 concerts, visited arenas throughout 2018.

The Joshua Tree Tour 2017 - 30th Anniversary

15 Countries / 52 Shows

The worldwide tour was staged to commemorate the 30th anniversary of their 1987 album The Joshua Tree. U2 originally wanted to play a set of one-off shows in the US and Europe to celebrate the anniversary, but ultimately decided to stage a full concert tour instead.

Innocence + Experience Tour 2015

13 Countries / 79 Shows

The Innocence + Experience Tour was staged in support of the band's 2014 album Songs of Innocence. The tour visited arenas throughout 2015. It was U2's first time playing arenas since 2005–2006 on their Vertigo Tour. Comprising two legs and 79 concerts, the Innocence + Experience Tour began on 14 May 2015 in Vancouver, Canada. It visited North America and Europe.

U2 360° Tour 2009 / 2010 / 2011

30 Countries / 110 Shows

The U2 360° tour visited stadiums in Europe, North America, Asia, Oceania, Africa and South America from 2009 to 2011 and comprised 110 shows. The concerts featured the band playing "in the round" on a circular stage, allowing the audience to surround them on all sides. To accommodate the stage configuration, a large four-legged structure nicknamed "The Claw" was built above the stage, with the sound system and a cylindrical, expanding video screen on top of it. At 50 meters tall, it was the largest stage ever constructed. U2 360° concluded in July 2011 as the highest-grossing concert tour (grossing $736 million) and the highest-attended concert tour (selling 7.3 million tickets).

Vertigo Tour 2005 / 2006

26 Countries / 129 Shows

Staged in support of the group's 2004 album How to Dismantle an Atomic Bomb, the tour visited arenas and stadiums from 2005 through 2006. The Vertigo Tour consisted of five legs that alternated between indoor arena shows in North America and outdoor stadium shows internationally.

Elevation Tour 2001

14 Countries / 113 Shows

In support of the group's 2000 album All That You Can't Leave Behind, the tour visited arenas across North America and Europe in 2001. Contrasting with the extravagant, outdoor productions of the band's previous two live ventures, the Zoo TV Tour (1992–1993) and the PopMart Tour (1997–1998), the Elevation Tour saw them return to indoor arenas with a much more stripped-down, intimate stage design. The stage featured a heart-shaped catwalk that encircled many audience members, and festival seating was offered in the United States for the first time in the group's history.

Popmart Tour 1997 / 1998

31 Countries / 91 Shows

Launched in support of the group's 1997 album Pop, the tour's concerts were performed in stadiums and parks in 1997 and 1998. PopMart was elaborately staged. Its lavish stage design had a 30 meter LED screen, a 50 meter golden arch, and a large mirror-ball lemon. The PopMart tour's five legs and 91 shows attracted about 3.9 million fans.

ZooTV Tour 1992 / 1993

19 Countries / 150 Shows

The tour visited arenas and stadiums from 1992 to 1993, in support of their 1991 album Achtung Baby. It was intended to mirror the group's new musical direction on Achtung Baby. In contrast to U2's austere stage setups from previous tours, the ZooTV Tour was an elaborately staged multimedia spectacle, satirising television and media oversaturation by attempting to instill "sensory overload" in its audience. To escape their reputation for being earnest and overly serious, U2 embraced a more lighthearted and self-deprecating image on tour. Zoo TV and Achtung Baby were central to the group's 1990s reinvention.

Lovetown Tour 1989 / 1990

12 Countries / 47 Shows

The Lovetown Tour took place in late 1989 and early 1990 following the release of the album Rattle and Hum. While the tour was limited in scope, it reached places that the Joshua Tree Tour had missed, all the while avoiding the United States entirely.

The Joshua Tree Tour 1987

11 Countries / 96 Shows

This tour's opening night was 2 April in Tempe, Arizona. The day before the opening night, Bono fell onto a spotlight he was carrying, cutting open his chin. Bono had partially lost his voice as a result. He asked the audience to help him sing the majority of the set, which they were happy to do.
The first leg took place in American indoor arenas during April and May. The second leg in European arenas and outdoor stadiums from late May through to early August. The third leg returned to American and Canadian arenas and stadiums in the autumn. The tour ended on 20 December back where it started in Tempe, Arizona.

The Unforgettable Fire Tour 1985 / 1986

14 Countries / 103 Shows

The tour took place in 1984 and 1985 in support of band's album The Unforgettable Fire. Beginning in August 1984 with the band's first tour to Australia and New Zealand, the tour spanned four further legs which included 43 concerts in Europe and 50 in North America.

War Tour 1983

9 Countries / 83 Shows

The tour took place in Western Europe, the United States, Canada and Japan, with new material from the group's third album War taking an increasing role as the tour progressed. Venues were mostly halls, but some arenas were introduced later on. U2's performances were very well received both critically and commercially, especially in the United States where U2 broke through to become a major act. Scenes of lead singer Bono waving a white flag during the song "Sunday Bloody Sunday" became an emblematic image of this phase of U2's career. It was their first tour as full-time headlining act and their first to be profitable.

October Tour 1981

7 Countries / 79 Shows

October tour took place in 1981 and 1982 to support the band's second studio album, October, which was released in October 1981. The tour followed a similar pattern to the previous tour and consisted of five legs, three in Europe and two in North America.

Boy Tour 1980

9 Countries / 68 Shows

The Boy tour was staged in support of their debut album Boy (1980), the tour visited small clubs from 1980–1981. Comprising five legs, the tour began on 6 September 1980 in Coventry, England and ended on 9 June 1981 in London, England. It was U2's first tour of continental Europe and North America.
